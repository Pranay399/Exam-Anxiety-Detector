# Empathy Map Canvas

## Project: AI-Based Exam Anxiety Detector
## User Persona: Final Year University Student

---

## Who Are We Empathizing With?

**Name:** Priya (Composite Student Persona)  
**Age:** 21  
**Background:** Final-year Engineering student preparing for semester exams  

---

## Empathy Map

### 👀 SEES
- Friends looking stressed and exhausted in the library
- Social media posts about others finishing their revision
- Exam timetable with multiple papers in quick succession
- News articles about student mental health and burnout
- Classmates who seem calm and confident

### 🎧 HEARS
- "You should have started revision earlier"
- "This paper will decide your GPA"
- "Everyone else seems so prepared"
- Advice from parents to study harder
- Friends discussing how tough the syllabus is

### 💭 THINKS & FEELS
- "What if I forget everything during the exam?"
- "I don't think I'm good enough"
- Fear of failure and disappointing family
- Overwhelmed by the amount of material
- Guilt about procrastinating
- Lonely and unable to talk about it

### 🗣️ SAYS
- "I'm fine, just a bit tired"
- "I'll start properly tomorrow"
- "I feel like I don't know anything"
- Avoids telling teachers or counselors about struggles
- Tells friends it's "not that bad"

### 😤 PAINS (Frustrations)
- Cannot objectively measure own anxiety level
- Doesn't know if what they're feeling is "normal"
- Available student support resources feel inaccessible or stigmatized
- Counseling appointments take weeks to schedule
- No immediate, private outlet for expressing pre-exam fears

### 🎯 GAINS (Needs & Goals)
- Needs to feel heard and understood
- Wants personalized, actionable advice — not generic platitudes
- Needs **private**, **instant** access to a support tool
- Wants to know their anxiety is recognized and valid
- Needs coping tips tailored to their specific anxiety level

---

## Key Insight from Empathy Map

> Students need a **private, instant, non-judgmental** tool that validates their feelings and gives them **specific actions** to take — not generic advice or another form to fill in.

This directly informed the design decisions:
- ✅ Simple one-input text box (low barrier)
- ✅ Color-coded results (immediately understandable)
- ✅ Tailored tips per anxiety level (actionable, not generic)
- ✅ No login or personal data required (privacy-first)
