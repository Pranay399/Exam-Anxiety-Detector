# Define Problem Statements

## Project Title
AI-Based Exam Anxiety Detector

---

## 1. Problem Overview

Students across universities and schools face significant exam-related anxiety that negatively impacts their academic performance, mental health, and overall well-being. Traditional systems of identifying struggling students rely on manual observation by teachers or self-reported surveys — both of which are slow, subjective, and often too late to be effective.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

> **"How might we use Artificial Intelligence to automatically detect and classify exam-related anxiety from student text inputs, enabling early intervention and personalized mental health support?"**

---

## 3. Who Is Affected?

| Stakeholder | Pain Point |
|---|---|
| Students | Cannot self-identify or articulate anxiety levels accurately |
| Teachers / Counselors | Lack tools to proactively monitor at-risk students |
| Academic Institutions | No data-driven system to track mental wellness trends |
| Parents | Unable to monitor their child's emotional state remotely |

---

## 4. Current Challenges

- **No real-time detection:** Current anxiety assessments are periodic, not continuous.
- **Subjectivity:** Human-based assessment is inconsistent across evaluators.
- **Low reporting rates:** Students under-report anxiety due to stigma.
- **Delayed intervention:** By the time anxiety is identified, it may have already impacted exam performance.
- **Generic solutions:** Available mental health tools are not tailored to exam-specific contexts.

---

## 5. Impact of the Problem

- 40–60% of students experience exam anxiety globally (APA research)
- Exam anxiety contributes to performance drops of up to 20% in standardized tests
- Unaddressed anxiety leads to burnout, dropout, and long-term mental health issues

---

## 6. Desired Outcome

An automated, real-time system that:
1. Accepts free-form text from students (reflections, pre-exam thoughts)
2. Classifies anxiety level as **Low**, **Moderate**, or **High**
3. Provides immediate, meaningful feedback and coping suggestions
4. Empowers educators with anonymized trend data for early intervention

---

## 7. Constraints & Assumptions

- Text input must be in English
- The system is not a replacement for professional psychological evaluation
- Students consent to their data being processed for wellness purposes
- The model is trained on publicly available mental health datasets
